Lemosho Route Overview
The Lemosho Route approaches Mount Kilimanjaro from the western side and is considered one of the most scenic and successful routes to the summit.
This route offers breathtaking views across the Shira Plateau, Cathedral Peak, and Shira Caldera before joining the Machame Route and following the famous Southern Circuit through Lava Tower, Barranco, and Barafu Camp.
Climbers experience low traffic during the early days of the trek, making it ideal for those seeking a quieter and more immersive mountain experience.

The minimum duration for the Lemosho Route is 7 days; however, the 8-day Lemosho Route is highly recommended for better acclimatization and a higher summit success rate.
Similar to the Machame Route, Lemosho is scenically spectacular and diverse, featuring lush rainforests, moorland, alpine desert, and glacial landscapes.
The route is also rich in wildlife, including black-and-white colobus monkeys, various bird species, and small jackals that may appear during the dry season from the Longido Game Controlled Area.

Machame Route Overview
The Machame Route, also known as the ‚ÄúWhiskey Route,‚Äù is one of the most popular and scenic routes to climb Mount Kilimanjaro.
The 7-day Machame Route itinerary is considered the ideal schedule, offering excellent acclimatization and a high summit success rate.
On this variation, the trek from Barranco to Barafu Camp is split into two days, allowing climbers to rest before the demanding summit night.
Since summit day begins around midnight, having a shorter trekking day beforehand helps climbers conserve energy and improve performance.
This itinerary is highly recommended and widely used by experienced and reputable Kilimanjaro operators.

DAY 1: Machame Gate to Machame Camp
Elevation: 5,400 ft to 9,400 ft
Distance: 11 km
Hiking Time: 5‚Äì7 hours
Habitat: Rain Forest
The drive from Moshi to Mount Kilimanjaro National Park takes approximately 50 minutes and passes through Machame village on the lower slopes of the mountain.
After registration at the gate, begin trekking through dense rainforest along a winding trail up a ridge.
Lower sections of the trail can be muddy and slippery, so gaiters and trekking poles are recommended.
Continue hiking until reaching Machame Camp for dinner and overnight.

DAY 2: Machame Camp to Shira Camp
Elevation: 9,400 ft to 12,500 ft
Distance: 5 km
Hiking Time: 4‚Äì6 hours
Habitat: Moorland
After breakfast, leave the rainforest and follow an ascending path across a small valley.
Walk along a steep rocky ridge covered with heather before the ridge ends.
The route then turns west into a river gorge leading to Shira Camp.
Dinner and overnight at Shira Camp.

DAY 3: Shira Camp to Lava Tower to Barranco Camp
Elevation: 12,500 ft to 13,000 ft
Distance: 10 km
Hiking Time: 6‚Äì8 hours
Habitat: Semi-Desert
From the Shira Plateau, trek east along a ridge toward Kibo Peak.
Turn southeast toward Lava Tower, also known as the ‚ÄúShark‚Äôs Tooth.‚Äù
After passing Lava Tower, continue to a junction leading toward Arrow Glacier at approximately 16,000 ft.
Descend to Barranco Camp at 13,000 ft for dinner and overnight.
Although you end the day at a similar elevation, this is a critical acclimatization day that prepares your body for summit night.

DAY 4: Barranco Camp to Karanga Camp
Elevation: 13,000 ft to 13,100 ft
Distance: 5 km
Hiking Time: 4‚Äì5 hours
Habitat: Alpine Desert
After breakfast, climb the famous Barranco Wall, a steep but non-technical scramble.
Continue along a ridge to Karanga Valley and Karanga Camp.
This is a short trekking day designed primarily for acclimatization.
Dinner and overnight at Karanga Camp.

DAY 5: Karanga Camp to Barafu Camp
Elevation: 13,100 ft to 15,300 ft
Distance: 4 km
Hiking Time: 4‚Äì5 hours
Habitat: Alpine Desert
After breakfast, leave Karanga Camp and join the Mweka Trail junction.
Continue ascending to Barafu Camp, completing the Southern Circuit of Kilimanjaro.
Enjoy panoramic views of the summit from multiple angles, including Mawenzi and Kibo peaks.
Dinner and early rest at Barafu Camp in preparation for summit night.

DAY 6: Barafu Camp to Uhuru Peak to Mweka Camp
Elevation: 15,300 ft to 19,345 ft, then down to 10,000 ft
Distance: 5 km ascent / 12 km descent
Hiking Time: 7‚Äì8 hours ascent / 4‚Äì6 hours descent
Habitat: Arctic Zone
Begin the summit attempt between midnight and 2:00 AM.
Ascend between the Rebmann and Ratzel glaciers toward Stella Point on the crater rim.
This section is the most physically and mentally demanding part of the climb.
At Stella Point (18,600 ft), take a short rest and enjoy a spectacular sunrise, weather permitting.
Continue for approximately one hour to Uhuru Peak, the highest point in Africa.
After photos at the summit, descend to Barafu Camp for lunch, then continue to Mweka Camp.
Dinner and overnight at Mweka Camp, located in the upper forest zone.

DAY 7: Mweka Camp to Mweka Gate to Moshi
Elevation: 10,000 ft to 5,400 ft
Distance: 10 km
Hiking Time: 3‚Äì4 hours
Habitat: Rain Forest
After breakfast, descend through rainforest to Mweka Park Gate.
Receive your summit certificates at the gate.

Best Months to Climb Kilimanjaro
Avoid long rains in March, April, and May.
November may have short rains but climbing is still possible.
The ideal months for climbing are January, February, August, and September.
July can be colder, while November to December may be wet.
January to February is generally the warmest with clear skies.
Our guides maintain outstanding success rates compared to the overall average for climbers.

Proper acclimatization is the key to summit success. Three key principles: go slowly ("Pole Pole"), stay well-hydrated, and consider taking Diamox under medical advice.

How to Avoid Altitude Sickness (AMS)
AMS occurs above 2,400 m due to low oxygen pressure. Symptoms can be severe and unpredictable. Avoid AMS by:
1. Walking high, sleeping low.
2. Moving slowly; maintain a conversational pace.
3. Drinking at least 3 liters of water daily.
4. Consulting a doctor about Diamox to aid acclimatization.

Effects of Low Atmospheric Pressure

Recognizing AMS
AMS can onset suddenly and severely. Guides monitor oxygen saturation and overall condition. Report any symptoms immediately.

Client Descent Protocol
