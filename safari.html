1 Day Safari

1 Day Arusha National Park Safari ‚Äì Tanzania Nature and Wildlife Experience

Arusha National Park was formerly known as Ngurdoto Crater Highland National Park until 1967.
The park currently covers an area of approximately 137 square kilometers (53 square miles).
It is strategically located near the town of Arusha, between the iconic Mount Kilimanjaro and Mount Meru.
Both the park and the town take their name from the Wa-Arusha people, who traditionally inhabit this region.

This unforgettable one-day safari excursion can depart from Arusha, Moshi, or Kilimanjaro International Airport (JRO).
During this tour, you will explore the spectacular Ngurdoto Crater, the scenic Momella Lakes, lush highland montane forests, and the rugged slopes of Mount Meru, which rises to 4,565 meters (14,977 feet).

Arusha National Park is rich in wildlife and offers excellent game viewing opportunities.
Wildlife commonly seen includes Colobus monkeys, velvet monkeys, bushbucks, buffalos, red forest duikers, hippos, elephants, and giraffes.
The park is also a paradise for bird lovers, with abundant and fascinating birdlife, especially waterfowl around the lakes.

Day 1 ‚Äì Arusha National Park Game Drive

In the morning, you will drive to Arusha National Park for a full day of game viewing and nature exploration.
The park is dominated by Mount Meru, Africa‚Äôs fifth-highest mountain, standing at 4,565 meters above sea level.
A wide variety of animal species can be seen during the game drive.
You can expect to spot hippos, giraffes, zebras, warthogs, waterbucks, and bushbucks.
Occasionally, more elusive wildlife such as reedbucks, leopards, and hyenas may also be sighted.

The Momella Lakes are alkaline in nature and provide a year-round breeding area for flamingos.
These lakes attract large numbers of water birds, making the park an excellent destination for birdwatching safaris in Tanzania.

5 Days Tanzania Camping Safari to Lake Manyara, Serengeti & Ngorongoro Crater

This 5-day Tanzania camping safari offers an unforgettable wildlife adventure through Lake Manyara National Park, Serengeti National Park, and the Ngorongoro Crater.
The itinerary is carefully designed to maximize game viewing, scenic landscapes, and cultural highlights while traveling in comfort with professional safari guides.

Day 0 ‚Äì Arrival in Arusha

Day 1 ‚Äì Arusha to Lake Manyara National Park

Distance: Approximately 30‚Äì50 kilometers.
Non-game viewing travel time: 3‚Äì4 hours.

After breakfast, the safari departs from Arusha.
Your first stop will be Arusha town for any last-minute shopping or personal purchases.
Afterwards, you will drive to Lake Manyara National Park, one of Tanzania‚Äôs most scenic parks.

Lake Manyara National Park is famous for its breathtaking landscapes and diverse habitats.
These include acacia woodlands, groundwater forests, baobab-strewn cliffs, algae-streaked hot springs, swamps, and the alkaline lake itself.
Due to this diversity, the park supports a large number of wildlife species and an impressive variety of birdlife.

Lake Manyara has the largest concentration of baboons in the world.
The park is also well known for its tree-climbing lions, a rare and fascinating sight.
Dinner and overnight stay will be at Ngorongoro Wildlife Lodge.

Day 2‚Äì3 ‚Äì Serengeti National Park

Distance: Approximately 145 kilometers.

After breakfast, you will depart for Serengeti National Park, traveling via the scenic highland farmlands of Karatu and the Ngorongoro Conservation Area.
Leaving the highlands behind, you will descend into the heart of wild Africa, the vast Serengeti National Park.
The Serengeti is famous for its endless plains stretching as far as the eye can see.

You will proceed to the central Serengeti area, known as the Seronera region.
This area is one of the richest wildlife habitats in the park and is centered around the Seronera River.
The river provides a permanent water source and attracts a wide variety of wildlife throughout the year.

You will arrive in time for lunch and enjoy an afternoon game drive in Serengeti National Park.

You will start the day with an early morning game drive in Serengeti National Park.
Afterwards, you will depart for the Ngorongoro Conservation Area.

En route, you will stop at Olduvai Gorge, one of the most important prehistoric sites in the world.
The gorge has a history dating back to the dawn of mankind.
It was here that Dr. Louis and Mary Leakey discovered the fossils of ‚ÄúNutcracker Man‚Äù and ‚ÄúHandy Man.‚Äù
These discoveries are considered vital links in the study of human evolution.

Day 5 ‚Äì Ngorongoro Crater to Arusha or Airport

Distance: Approximately 210 kilometers.
Non-game viewing travel time: 3‚Äì4 hours.

After an early breakfast, you will descend over 600 meters into the Ngorongoro Crater for wildlife viewing.
The crater is supported by a year-round water supply and rich grazing grounds.
This allows it to support a vast variety of animals throughout the year.

Wildlife commonly seen includes wildebeest, zebras, buffaloes, elands, warthogs, hippos, and giant African elephants.
The crater is also famous for its dense population of predators such as lions, hyenas, jackals, cheetahs, and the elusive leopard.
Spotting a leopard often requires a trained and patient eye.

You will also visit Lake Magadi, a shallow alkaline lake located in the southwestern corner of the crater.
This lake attracts large numbers of flamingos, hippos, and other water birds.

In the late afternoon, you will transfer back to Arusha for dinner and overnight accommodation or airport drop-off.

PUMZIKO SAFARI ‚Äì 9 Days Tanzania Luxury Wildlife and Cultural Safari

The Pumziko Safari 9 Days Tanzania tour offers a perfect blend of wildlife adventures, cultural encounters, walking safaris, and scenic landscapes.
This carefully crafted itinerary explores Arusha National Park, Tarangire National Park, Lake Manyara, Lake Eyasi, Ngorongoro Crater, Serengeti National Park, and Lake Natron.
It is ideal for travelers seeking a relaxed yet immersive luxury safari experience in Tanzania.

Day 1 ‚Äì Arusha to Arusha National Park

After breakfast, you will be met by our professional safari driver-guide for a short safari briefing.
You will then travel east to Arusha National Park, the closest national park to Arusha town and Kilimanjaro International Airport.
Despite its small size, Arusha National Park is one of the most scenic and topographically diverse parks in Tanzania.

The park is known for abundant wildlife and breathtaking landscapes.
Its three main highlights are Mount Meru, Tanzania‚Äôs second-highest peak at 4,566 meters, the colorful Momela Lakes, and the 3-kilometer-wide Ngurdoto Crater.
Game viewing and nature exploration will be enjoyed throughout the day.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Green Mountain Lodge.

Day 2 ‚Äì Arusha to Tarangire National Park

After breakfast, you will check out from the hotel and depart for Tarangire National Park.
Upon arrival, you will begin with a guided bush walk followed by a gentle but scenic climb to the top of a nearby hill.
After descending, you will enjoy lunch before continuing by vehicle to the next camp.

During the drive, you will pass through remote Maasai settlements.
You will visit a local Maasai chief to pay respects and learn about their traditions and way of life.
In the afternoon, you will enjoy a walking safari inside Tarangire National Park.
This area is rich in herbivores such as giraffes, impalas, dik-diks, and other wildlife.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Fanaka Lodge.

Day 3 ‚Äì Full Day Game Drive at Lake Manyara National Park

Early in the morning, you will have breakfast before driving to Lake Manyara National Park for a full-day game drive.
En route, you will pass Maasai herders with their cattle and goats and drive through Mto wa Mbu village.
This lively village is home to more than 120 tribes and is known for its colorful cultural atmosphere.

Inside Lake Manyara National Park, you will begin game viewing through lush groundwater forests.
The drive continues to open grasslands and along the lake shore.
Lake Manyara is a birdwatcher‚Äôs paradise with many endemic and migratory bird species.
Wildlife sightings may include elephants, impalas, bushbucks, hippos, dik-diks, and the famous tree-climbing lions.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Fanaka Lodge.

Day 4 ‚Äì Lake Manyara to Lake Eyasi

After breakfast, you will drive to the shores of Lake Eyasi.
Lake Eyasi is a scenic soda lake located on the southern border of the Ngorongoro Conservation Area.
It lies along the western Rift Valley wall at the base of the Eyasi Escarpment.

You will meet the Hadzabe tribe, one of the last remaining hunter-gatherer communities in Africa.
Together with the Nilotic-speaking Datoga tribe, they live around Lake Eyasi.
You will visit their homesteads, learn about their traditional lifestyles, track animals, identify medicinal plants, and experience their daily survival skills.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Lake Eyasi Lodge.

Day 5 ‚Äì Lake Eyasi to Ngorongoro Conservation Area

After breakfast, you will drive to the Ngorongoro Conservation Area entrance gate.
From the gate, you will proceed to the crater rim and enjoy spectacular panoramic views of the Ngorongoro Crater from above.

You will descend into the crater floor for a full day of game drives.
Ngorongoro Crater is one of the largest intact volcanic calderas in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
It is home to the Big Five, including rhinos, lions, leopards, buffaloes, and elephants.
Other wildlife includes zebras, wildebeest, hippos, and hyenas.

Birdlife is equally impressive, with flamingos at the soda lake, as well as geese, storks, vultures, and many other bird species.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Rhino Lodge.

Day 6 ‚Äì Ngorongoro Crater to Serengeti National Park

After breakfast, you will begin a scenic drive to Serengeti National Park.
Leaving the highlands behind, you descend into the heart of wild Africa.
The Serengeti is famous for its endless plains stretching to the horizon.
Game viewing opportunities increase as you enter the park.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Serengeti Heritage.

Day 7 ‚Äì Full Day Game Drive in Serengeti National Park

After breakfast, you will enjoy a full day game drive in Serengeti National Park.
The game drive will focus on the Retima River area, located north of Seronera.
This river system attracts large numbers of wildlife, including hippos and crocodiles that spend much of the day in the water.
Predators such as lions, leopards, and cheetahs are also commonly seen in this region.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Serengeti Heritage.

Day 8 ‚Äì Serengeti National Park to Lake Natron

Depending on arrival time, you will enjoy an evening walk to the southern shores of Lake Natron.
Lake Natron is an ecologically unique destination and one of the most important breeding sites for flamingos in East Africa.
Nearly two million flamingos migrate here each year to nest on the salty crust formed during the dry season.

Your guide will explain the lake‚Äôs unique flora and fauna, as well as the cultural traditions, rituals, and customs of the Maasai people.
The Maasai around Lake Natron maintain a semi-nomadic lifestyle in this remote region.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Lake Natron.

Day 9 ‚Äì Lake Natron to Arusha

In the morning, you will enjoy bird watching around Lake Natron.
This area is one of the best destinations in Tanzania for flamingo viewing and other bird species.
Bird walks usually take place during the morning and evening hours.

You may also enjoy a guided hike to the Engare Sero Waterfalls.
The hike offers excellent photo opportunities and stunning views of Lake Natron from above.
After lunch, you will drive back to Arusha, marking the end of your safari adventure.

En route, you will pass Maasai herders with their cattle and goats and drive through Mto wa Mbu village.
This lively village is home to more than 120 tribes and is known for its colorful cultural atmosphere.

Inside Lake Manyara National Park, you will begin game viewing through lush groundwater forests.
The drive continues to open grasslands and along the lake shore.
Lake Manyara is a birdwatcher‚Äôs paradise with many endemic and migratory bird species.
Wildlife sightings may include elephants, impalas, bushbucks, hippos, dik-diks, and the famous tree-climbing lions.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Fanaka Lodge.

Day 4 ‚Äì Lake Manyara to Lake Eyasi

After breakfast, you will drive to the shores of Lake Eyasi.
Lake Eyasi is a scenic soda lake located on the southern border of the Ngorongoro Conservation Area.
It lies along the western Rift Valley wall at the base of the Eyasi Escarpment.

You will meet the Hadzabe tribe, one of the last remaining hunter-gatherer communities in Africa.
Together with the Nilotic-speaking Datoga tribe, they live around Lake Eyasi.
You will visit their homesteads, learn about their traditional lifestyles, track animals, identify medicinal plants, and experience their daily survival skills.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Lake Eyasi Lodge.

Day 5 ‚Äì Lake Eyasi to Ngorongoro Conservation Area

After breakfast, you will drive to the Ngorongoro Conservation Area entrance gate.
From the gate, you will proceed to the crater rim and enjoy spectacular panoramic views of the Ngorongoro Crater from above.

You will descend into the crater floor for a full day of game drives.
Ngorongoro Crater is one of the largest intact volcanic calderas in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
It is home to the Big Five, including rhinos, lions, leopards, buffaloes, and elephants.
Other wildlife includes zebras, wildebeest, hippos, and hyenas.

Birdlife is equally impressive, with flamingos at the soda lake, as well as geese, storks, vultures, and many other bird species.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Rhino Lodge.

Day 6 ‚Äì Ngorongoro Crater to Serengeti National Park

After breakfast, you will begin a scenic drive to Serengeti National Park.
Leaving the highlands behind, you descend into the heart of wild Africa.
The Serengeti is famous for its endless plains stretching to the horizon.
Game viewing opportunities increase as you enter the park.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Serengeti Heritage.

Day 7 ‚Äì Full Day Game Drive in Serengeti National Park

After breakfast, you will enjoy a full day game drive in Serengeti National Park.
The game drive will focus on the Retima River area, located north of Seronera.
This river system attracts large numbers of wildlife, including hippos and crocodiles that spend much of the day in the water.
Predators such as lions, leopards, and cheetahs are also commonly seen in this region.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Serengeti Heritage.

Day 8 ‚Äì Serengeti National Park to Lake Natron

Depending on arrival time, you will enjoy an evening walk to the southern shores of Lake Natron.
Lake Natron is an ecologically unique destination and one of the most important breeding sites for flamingos in East Africa.
Nearly two million flamingos migrate here each year to nest on the salty crust formed during the dry season.

Your guide will explain the lake‚Äôs unique flora and fauna, as well as the cultural traditions, rituals, and customs of the Maasai people.
The Maasai around Lake Natron maintain a semi-nomadic lifestyle in this remote region.
Dinner and overnight accommodation will be at Lake Natron.

Day 9 ‚Äì Lake Natron to Arusha

In the morning, you will enjoy bird watching around Lake Natron.
This area is one of the best destinations in Tanzania for flamingo viewing and other bird species.
Bird walks usually take place during the morning and evening hours.

You may also enjoy a guided hike to the Engare Sero Waterfalls.
The hike offers excellent photo opportunities and stunning views of Lake Natron from above.
After lunch, you will drive back to Arusha, marking the end of your safari adventure.
